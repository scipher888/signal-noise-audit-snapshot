The editorial path

Six gates between an idea and publication.

The same model may perform several internal roles, so these are not independent inspectors. The value lies in explicit constraints, recorded decisions, and the ability to route work backward—or stop it.

The human author is in the loop throughout. They initiate the work before admission, edit and approve between the gates, and give the final edit and approval after outside review.

What the system is for

Constraints, protections, and limits.

The structure is meant to keep confidence from outrunning evidence. It does not make the work safe, correct, or true by virtue of existing.

It constrains

What a draft may do

  • No drafting without a plausible outside anchor.
  • No hiding uncertainty.
  • Every load-bearing claim answers to something beyond coherence.
  • Narrative confidence cannot exceed evidentiary confidence.
  • Polish may not change epistemic content.
  • Outside input is advisory, never authoritative.
It tries to prevent

Predictable forms of drift

  • Coherence theater.
  • Polish standing in for thinking.
  • A compelling story outrunning evidence.
  • An external reviewer becoming the author.
  • Confidence being upgraded under cover of clarity.
  • Personhood ambiguity around AI.
It cannot guarantee

Truth or independence

  • A shared model blind spot can survive every role.
  • A confident error can pass.
  • A critique can fail in the same way as an answer.
  • No internal process substitutes for outside-world evidence.
  • A recorded trace is only as complete as what was captured.
A

The admission rule

A thesis needs a plausible outside anchor: evidence, a testable prediction, a real cost, accountability, or concrete specificity. Without one, the piece is held, recast, or not published.

Open a role

What each check is allowed to change.

The cards below separate the role, the constraint, the failure route, and the remaining risk. Open them in any order—one at a time or all at once.

1 BuilderDrafts the thesis and structure.
Job

Turn the originating tension into a useful, readable argument with a clear thesis and sequence.

Constraint

May optimize usefulness and readability, but may not hide uncertainty or manufacture support.

Failure route

Buried hedges, unsupported confidence, or overclaiming route the draft back for revision.

What it prevents

Polish standing in for thinking before the argument is even stress-tested.

Remaining risk

This is the least adversarial role. The same model can carry an early error into every later stage.

2 Critic / AdversaryAttacks assumptions, mechanisms, evidence, and scope.
Job

Use a fixed question battery to seek alternatives, missing evidence, scope errors, and unfalsifiable framing.

Constraint

Challenge the argument rather than merely improving its language or supplying a more persuasive version.

Failure route

If no credible alternative has been considered, the claim is flagged rather than waved through.

What it prevents

Cherry-picking, one-explanation lock-in, and frameworks that appear to explain every possible outcome.

Remaining risk

A same-model critic can share the builder’s blind spot. An optional cross-model panel exists, but is experimental and not used for every issue.

3 RefereeRules claim by claim and enforces bright lines.
Job

Keep, qualify, downgrade, remove, fact-check, or issue a no-publish decision. When roles disagree, the referee controls the mode and language.

Constraint

Narrative force cannot be used to raise confidence beyond what the record can support.

Failure route

Unsupported confidence must be qualified, downgraded, cut, or sent back for evidence.

What it prevents

A compelling story outrunning the available evidence.

Remaining risk

The system is still partly self-adjudicating. A referee role does not create independence when it shares the same model and evidence.

4 EditorMakes the approved argument readable.
Job

Compress, sequence, and clarify the approved argument while preserving its epistemic shape.

Constraint

May not upgrade confidence, remove a hedge, weaken a warrant, or drop a caveat.

Failure route

Any polish that changes the strength of a claim routes the passage back to the referee.

What it prevents

Readability quietly strengthening the claim under cover of smoother prose.

Remaining risk

Connotation can drift even when literal wording seems preserved. A smooth error can become harder to notice.

5 Outside narrative reviewA separate near-final read focused on clarity and drift.
Job

Read the near-final piece from outside the internal sequence and identify narrative problems, ambiguity, or hidden drift.

Constraint

May improve prose only. Changes to claims, confidence, caveats, or transparency must route back through the process.

Failure route

A wholesale rewrite is rejected when it flattens the voice or erases the decision trail; local edits may be accepted.

What it prevents

An outside reviewer becoming the author or upgrading claims under the cover of polish.

Remaining risk

A persuasive rewrite can still smuggle in drift. Independence is reduced when outside review uses the same underlying model in a different role and prompt.

Bright lines that force a hold or rework.

The referee is instructed not to publish—or to force substantial revision—when any of these conditions remain:

  • An unfalsifiable framework is presented as truth.
  • No credible alternative explanation has been considered.
  • Narrative confidence exceeds evidentiary confidence.
  • The argument can explain any outcome and therefore risks explaining none.
  • Process claims are not inspectable.

Where independence exists—and where it does not.

The builder, critic, referee, and editor are often roles played by one AI model. Different prompts and decision rights may create useful friction, but they do not create independent AI cognition.

The optional cross-model AI panel and outside AI review are the only points intended to add a separate perspective. Even then, outside review may use the same AI model in a different context.

Practical implication: more weight belongs on outside anchors, recorded changes, reader challenge, and correction—not on the number of internal passes.

What does this record establish?

It establishes the intended constraints, role boundaries, and known failure routes as of this dated snapshot. It makes the process inspectable only to the degree that traces are actually recorded.

It does not establish that any published claim is true, that the checks worked in a particular case, or that the system is free of shared blind spots.

A map, not a certification of truth. These records expose choices, constraints, and open questions. Trust is useful here only when it leads to closer inspection rather than closure.
