Am I Building?

Origin

The issue began with a hopeful building impulse. Review narrowed it to what could actually be supported.

The short version

This issue started from a desire to know whether hours spent learning to build with AI are producing something meaningful, or only the feeling of it.

The draft began more hopeful and less guarded. Three adversarial passes and an outside review narrowed it: the feeling of progress is not evidence, and the practical question is not only whether the work exists but what it is costing the people closest to you.

After publication, a reader objection sharpened the central question from "is it real?" to "is it meaningful?" The public text changed in response.

Where it came from

What it asks of the reader

Go in with your eyes open. Learning to build with AI can be genuinely worth doing, and it can also pull harder than almost anything, in ways that quietly cost time with the people closest to you.

A map, not a certification of truth. These records expose choices, constraints, and open questions. Trust is useful here only when it leads to closer inspection rather than closure.
