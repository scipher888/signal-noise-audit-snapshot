How Issue 14 was built — the live attribution log

Signal & Noise Issue 14, “Am I Building?”, was drafted through the editorial process and logged as it went. Each entry below is one recorded change to the essay: what prompted it, and what changed in response. Distribution and outreach steps are left out — this is the editorial arc only.

Before you read

This is the audit-grade companion to Issue 14’s Structure and Reference records. Unlike the Reality Compiler conversation — a reconstructed, illustrative record — this was logged live, at the time each change was made.

The author bubbles are the author’s own words, quoted as recorded. AI editorial process bubbles are a logged summary of what changed in response — not verbatim AI text. Where a change came from a working step rather than a direct request, the AI bubble opens with a small “Prompted by…” note. The AI is a tool in an editorial process, not an author.

It shows where the author steered and where the AI executed. It is not proof the essay is right — that is what the Structure and Reference records test, and even they show constraint, not proof.

Early beta — the human side is heavily clipped. This first iteration captures the author’s words only where they were short and directly quotable; longer or descriptive input is compressed to a brief “Prompted by…” line, and a few entries show almost nothing of what the author actually said (a passing “Last potential edit I can think of…”, for instance). So it under-represents the human contribution — the author said far more than appears here. The AI-side summaries are fuller; a later version will surface more of the human input.
